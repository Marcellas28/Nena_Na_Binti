Sexual Health

Information about STIs, prevention, testing, and treatment options. Learn about safe practices and maintaining sexual health.

What are common STIs and their symptoms?

Common STIs include:

  • Chlamydia - Often asymptomatic, may cause discharge
  • Gonorrhea - May cause discharge, burning sensation
  • HPV - Can cause warts, often no symptoms
  • Herpes - Causes sores or blisters

Regular testing is important as many STIs show no symptoms.

How often should I get tested?

Testing frequency depends on your sexual activity:

  • Annually if sexually active
  • Every 3-6 months if multiple partners
  • After each new sexual partner
  • Immediately if experiencing symptoms

Family Planning

Explore different contraception methods, fertility awareness, and family planning resources.

What contraception options are available?

Available contraception methods include:

  • Hormonal (pills, patches, injections)
  • Long-acting reversible (IUD, implants)
  • Barrier methods (condoms, diaphragms)
  • Natural family planning methods
How effective are different methods?

Effectiveness rates:

  • IUD/Implants: 99%+ effective
  • Birth Control Pills: 91-99% effective
  • Condoms: 85-98% effective
  • Natural Methods: 76-88% effective

Pregnancy

Information about conception, pregnancy health, prenatal care, and pregnancy options.

What are early pregnancy symptoms?
  • Missed period
  • Morning sickness
  • Breast tenderness
  • Fatigue
  • Frequent urination
When should I start prenatal care?

Prenatal care should begin:

  • As soon as pregnancy is confirmed
  • Ideally within first 8 weeks
  • Even before conception for pre-pregnancy planning

Why Us..

The 2014 Kenya Demographic and Health Survey (KDHS) found that on average half of men and women start engaging in sex at age 17 and 18, respectively.

In addition, 11% of girls and 20% of boys aged 15-19 start engaging in sex by age 15. One in five (23%) girls age 15-19 has an unmet need for contraceptives. Consequently, 1 in 5 girls aged 15-19 has begun childbearing and about half (47%) of these births are unwanted. The STI prevalence among girls aged 15-19 is 3 times the rate in boys of the same age group. Knowledge of comprehensive HIV prevention, HIV testing, and condom use are also low among adolescents relative to other age groups.

Sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV) SGBV is linked to high rates of unintended pregnancy, STIs including HIV, and psychosocial problems. The 2014 KDHS found that 6.5% of girls aged 15-19 and 2.7% of boys aged 15-19 have ever experienced sexual violence. Whereas, 32% of girls aged 15-19 and 42% of boys have ever experienced physical violence since age 15 years.

Every day in Kenya 7 women die from unsafe abortions which can be prevented. Unsafe abortion in Kenya is among the highest in Africa. Maternal mortality is high at about 6,000 deaths per year, 17% of them from complications of unsafe abortion
Therefore, the hotline seeks to create a safe space where adolescent girls and young women can have uninterrupted access to comprehensive SRHR information and services in a confidential and non-judgmental including access to safe abortion services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about reproductive health

How confidential are the consultations?

All consultations are completely confidential. We use secure, encrypted communications and follow strict privacy protocols. You can choose to remain anonymous, and no information is shared without your explicit consent.

What qualifications do your specialists have?

Our specialists are licensed healthcare professionals with specific training in reproductive health. They include gynecologists, reproductive health nurses, certified counselors, and sexual health educators.

How quickly can I speak with a specialist?

We typically respond to consultation requests within 24 hours. For urgent matters, our chat service is available during business hours for immediate support. Emergency services are available 24/7 through our partner clinics.

Is there a cost for consultations?

Basic information and resources are free. Consultation fees vary based on the type and duration of service. We offer sliding scale fees and can provide information about insurance coverage and financial assistance programs.
